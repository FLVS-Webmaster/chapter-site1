What Is TSA?

TSA, or Technology Student Association, is a nationwide competition for students spanning a broad range of interests and passions through a diversity of events. It fosters a love of STEM among students, and provides an environment where they can incorporate their individual skills into a modern context.


Motto:

ABOUT OUR CHAPTER


The TSA chapter at Boiling Springs High School is a Region 3 chapter apart of the Pennsylvania TSA organization. The chapter meets during a specified resource (study hall) period, and also holds after-school work sessions so members can spend time working on their projects with advisors there to assist them as needed. The chapter is mainly run by the youth-run officer team, which is elected by the members of the chapter. Currently this year there are 8 officers. The advisors are there to help and assist members with their projects and make sure that the chapter is successfully run, but the officer team organizes the meetings and makes sure that they are executed. Currently, the Boiling Springs HS chapter has a total of 28 registered members.



CONFERENCES


REGIONALS


Every year on the first Saturday in February, members of chapters apart of Region 3 come together to compete. The PA-TSA organization is split into different regions, that have their own conferences. At Regionals, this is the premilimary round where everyone starts. Members compete in different events they select, following the event guidelines, here and can qualify to States.

STATES


If members do well enough at their Regional conference, they can qualify to the State conference. For Boiling Springs, the minimum requirement to make it to States is either get a first place in at least one (1) event or place top three (3) in at least two events. The State conference for PA is held at Seven Springs Mountain Resort, outside of Somerset Pennsylvania.


NATIONALS


Nationals is the top conference members can make it to. It is difficult to make it to this conference. In our chapter, less than 3 people will move on from States to Nationals. The conference is held in a different place every year. This year it will be held in Nashville, while last year was held in Atlanta. To make it to Nationals, members must place (getting a 1st place or getting in the top 3) in three (3) events.
